The Healthcare Crisis

108 million patients worldwide are lost to follow-up due to lack of communication after hospital discharge. In the United States alone, 6 million adults fall through the cracks of post-discharge care. This failure in patient follow-up leads to preventable complications, readmissions, and deathsâ€”with particularly severe impacts in cancer care (49.7% patient loss), emergency departments (36.8%), and general surgery (31%). CareTrackr addresses this crisis by automating intelligent, personalized patient follow-up at scale.

My Contribution: AI Infrastructure

As the AI Infrastructure Lead, I researched and evaluated multiple LLM API providers to design a high-performance multi-agent architecture optimized for real-time processing. The challenge was unprecedented: analyze live patient phone calls, evaluate symptom severity across multiple medical domains, flag abnormalities, and generate physician reportsâ€”all while the conversation is happening. Traditional sequential LLM approaches would take 30-60 seconds, making real-time feedback impossible.

I architected a parallel processing system that orchestrates 17 specialized medical agents simultaneously. Each agent focuses on a specific medical domainâ€”cardiac symptoms, respiratory issues, pain assessment, medication compliance, mobility, mental health indicators, etc. Using ChatGPT 3.5 Turbo as the foundation, I implemented an orchestrator-validation pattern with error handling and response merging. The system achieves sub-5-second total processing time through intelligent parallelization, allowing the AI to provide feedback while patients are still on the call.

System Architecture & Workflow

CareTrackr automatically contacts patients post-discharge using verified hospital phone numbers via Twilio integration. The system conducts standardized medical questionnaires using conversational AI powered by OpenAI's Whisper for speech-to-text processing. Patient responses are simultaneously analyzed by the 17-agent infrastructure I designed. Each agent returns structured assessments with confidence scores, severity ratings, and specific concerns.

The orchestrator validates responses, the merger consolidates findings into a coherent clinical picture, and error validation ensures reliability. The system generates instant, auto-formatted PDF physician reports that integrate directly with hospital EHR systems. Based on flagged abnormalities, patients receive follow-up contact within 24-48 hours. This end-to-end automation transforms what was a manual, error-prone process into a reliable, scalable system.
