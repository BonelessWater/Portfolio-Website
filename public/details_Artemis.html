Project information

  • Project date: 2024
  • Project URL: github.com/BonelessWater/Artemis

Inspiration

As an avid outdoor enthusiast, I recognized the need for a comprehensive hiking companion that works even in areas with limited connectivity. Too many hikers venture into the wilderness unprepared, lacking access to survival information when they need it most. Artemis was born from the desire to create a safety-first app that also rewards users for contributing to environmental research.

Explanation

Artemis is a cross-platform mobile app built with React Native and Flask that serves as an advanced hiking companion. The app features real-time SOS alerts for emergency situations, offline survival guides powered by LLM capabilities for when cellular service is unavailable, and real-time friend tracking for group safety. Users can share environmental data like trail conditions, wildlife sightings, and weather observations, contributing to sustainable research while earning financial compensation. The gamification system includes peak achievements, trail completion badges, and challenges, which increased user engagement by 40% and boosted average session duration by 15 minutes.

Takeaways

This project expanded my mobile development skills and taught me the importance of designing for offline functionality. Implementing LLM capabilities for offline survival guides required creative solutions for data caching and compression. I learned how gamification can drive user engagement while simultaneously collecting valuable environmental data. The experience of building a location-based app also deepened my understanding of GPS integration, battery optimization, and real-time data synchronization across devices.
